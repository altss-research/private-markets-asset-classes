# Infrastructure

Infrastructure refers to investments in essential physical and organizational assets that support economic activity and public services, typically characterized by long-lived assets and stable, predictable cash flows.

## Characteristics

- Long-duration assets with extended useful lives  
- Cash flows often supported by contracts, regulation, or monopolistic positioning  
- Lower demand sensitivity relative to economic cycles  
- Capital-intensive development and maintenance requirements  
- Potential inflation linkage through regulated pricing or contractual escalators  

## Common Structures

- Brownfield and greenfield investment vehicles  
- Core and value-add infrastructure funds  
- Public-private partnerships (PPPs)  
- Asset-level concessions and operating companies  

## Common Allocator Use Cases

- Long-term income generation  
- Inflation protection  
- Portfolio diversification  
- Liability matching for long-duration capital
