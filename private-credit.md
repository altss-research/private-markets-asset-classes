# Private Credit

Private Credit refers to non-bank lending strategies where capital is provided directly to companies or asset owners outside of traditional public debt markets.

## Characteristics

- Contractual return profile based on interest income and fees  
- Seniority and structural protections such as collateral and covenants  
- Private, negotiated loan terms  
- Typically floating-rate instruments  
- Lower volatility relative to equity strategies, but limited liquidity  

## Common Structures

- Direct lending funds  
- Unitranche facilities  
- Mezzanine and subordinated debt  
- Specialty finance and asset-backed lending  
- NAV and fund-level lending structures  

## Common Allocator Use Cases

- Income generation  
- Downside risk mitigation within private-market portfolios  
- Diversification from equity-driven return streams  
- Reduced duration exposure relative to public fixed income
