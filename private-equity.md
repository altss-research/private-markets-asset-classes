# Private Equity

Private Equity refers to investment strategies focused on acquiring ownership stakes in private companies with the objective of long-term value creation.

## Characteristics

- Control or significant influence over portfolio companies
- Multi-year investment horizon
- Active governance and operational involvement
- Capital returned through exits such as sales, IPOs, or recapitalizations

## Common Structures

- Buyout funds
- Growth equity funds
- Special situations funds
- Sector-focused private equity vehicles

## Allocator Use

Institutional allocators use private equity to pursue:

- Long-term capital appreciation
- Illiquidity premiums
- Active value creation beyond public markets

Private equity exposure is typically accessed through closed-end fund structures with defined investment and harvesting periods.
